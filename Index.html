Read the following situation and answer questions 16 and 17

Rosa is teaching a lesson on greetings and classroom expressions to beginner students. She designs the following sequence:

  • Rosa models a sentence: “Good morning, teacher!” and the whole class repeats it in chorus three times.
  • She then changes one word and asks students to repeat again: “Good afternoon, teacher!”, “Good evening, teacher!”
  • Students practice in pairs, repeating the same patterns without variation.
  • Rosa corrects any deviation from the model immediately, asking students to repeat the correct form until it sounds natural.

Question 16

Which teaching methodology is Rosa applying?

Correct answer: a — Explanation

La secuencia de Rosa presenta los rasgos definitorios del ALM (Fries & Lado, 1957): modelo del docente → repetición coral → ejercicio de sustitución (cambiar una palabra del patrón) → práctica en pares sin variación real de contenido. No hay information gap ni propósito comunicativo genuino, por lo que no es CLT ni TBL. (Fries & Lado, 1957)

Situation (continued) — Rosa’s greetings lesson

Recall that Rosa corrects any deviation from the model immediately, asking students to repeat the correct form until it sounds natural.

Question 17

Why does Rosa correct errors immediately instead of waiting until the end of the activity?

Correct answer: c — Explanation

El ALM se fundamenta en el conductismo de Skinner (1957): el idioma se aprende como un conjunto de hábitos mecánicos. Si un error no se corrige de inmediato, se “fija” como un hábito incorrecto difícil de eliminar después. Esto es opuesto al delayed feedback del CLT y al Language Focus inductivo del TBL. (Skinner, 1957; Fries & Lado, 1957)

Read the following situation and answer questions 18, 19 and 20

Fernando teaches a lesson based on a short story in English. He designs the following sequence:

  • He writes the story on the board and translates it sentence by sentence into Spanish, asking students to copy both versions in their notebooks.
  • Students memorize a list of 15 new vocabulary words with their Spanish translations.
  • Fernando explains, in Spanish, the grammatical rule for the past tense used in the story, using sentence diagrams on the board.
  • As homework, students must translate five sentences from Spanish into English using the vocabulary they memorized.

Question 18

Which methodology does Fernando’s sequence represent?

Correct answer: b — Explanation

El Grammar-Translation Method (Richards & Rodgers, 2001), cuyos exponentes históricos incluyen a Seidenstücker, Plotz, Ollendorff y Meidinger, fue diseñado para enseñar lenguas clásicas como el latín. Su transposición al inglés mantiene la misma lógica: dominar la gramática y la literatura de la L2 a través de la L1, sin priorizar la comunicación oral ni situaciones reales. (Richards & Rodgers, 2001)

Situation (continued) — Fernando’s translation lesson

Consider the overall balance of skills practiced throughout Fernando’s sequence: reading, translating, memorizing, and writing homework sentences.

Question 19

Which language skill is MOST clearly neglected in Fernando’s lesson?

Correct answer: c — Explanation

El Grammar-Translation Method prioriza la lectura, la traducción y la gramática escrita, dejando la habilidad oral prácticamente sin trabajar. En ningún momento de la secuencia de Fernando los estudiantes hablan en inglés ni practican pronunciación o interacción oral. (Richards & Rodgers, 2001)

Situation (continued) — Fernando’s translation lesson

A colleague argues that Fernando’s lesson would be poorly evaluated in the MINEDU promotion exam.

Question 20

Which of the following BEST explains why?

Correct answer: a — Explanation

El MINEDU evalúa competencia comunicativa (Hymes, 1972; Canale & Swain, 1980), no solo corrección gramatical aislada. Una secuencia como la de Fernando — centrada en traducir y memorizar — no genera ninguna evidencia de que el estudiante pueda comunicarse en inglés en una situación real, que es precisamente el criterio que el examen de ascenso privilegia. (Hymes, 1972; Canale & Swain, 1980)
